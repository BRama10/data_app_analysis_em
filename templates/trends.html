Graphs & Charts

Graph Type 1 - Univariate Data

This type of graph is useful for determining traits of a single variable. In the example, the Energy Problems column is used. The graph takes the already distilled data and represents it as a bar chart, showing the breakdowns of each problem. THis kind of graph is especially useful when trying to determine what specific problem is the most apparent throughout EM serviced apartments.

Graph Type 2 - Multivariate Data

The most useful graph for analyzing the data as a whole is multivariate graphs such as double-columned bar graphs, scatter plots & matrices, heatmaps, and even violin plots. In the example, the thermostat temperatures for winter and summer are compared to each other with respect to the number of members in the household.

From the graph, we can see that there is a loose correlation between household size and thermostat temperatures. Additionally, the thermostat temperatures in the summer seem to be directly related to the winter temperatures.

Our main goal is to eventually present our findings, and show some kind of impact. A line graph, or even multivariate scatter matrix, can suffice. By combining various variables in multiple categories, we can visually depict trends in an easy manner.
